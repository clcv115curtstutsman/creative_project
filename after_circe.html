Advice from the Enchantress

With a respectful nod, you decline the goblet. Circe studies you for a moment, her gaze piercing yet not unkind. Then, to your surprise, she smiles. "Very well," she says, setting the goblet aside. "Your path requires boldness," she says thoughtfully. "Odysseus must journey to the land of the dead. Only there can he speak with the blind prophet Tiresias, who holds the key to your path home. To reach the underworld, sail to the far West of the World. There, you must offer sacrifices to the shades and summon Tiresias's spirit." Her words hang in the air, heavy with ominous undertones. You thank her as a sense of unease settles in your heart. As you leave, Circe points you towards a hidden cove where your ship awaits. The sea before you is calm, but the knowledge of your next destination fills you with a foreboding sense of adventure. The underworld awaits.
